📚 Political Wisdom Library

The Political Wisdom Library is a curated educational collection of quotations from political philosophers, jurists, statesmen, world leaders, Filipino heroes, public officials, and influential political thinkers. Quotations are compiled from original writings, speeches, public records, and reputable historical and academic references. Where appropriate, quotations are cross-referenced with authoritative primary or secondary sources to promote accuracy. Some quotations may be simplified or abbreviated for educational presentation while preserving their intended meaning.

  • Original books, essays, speeches, letters, and public writings of political thinkers and leaders
  • Works of Plato, Aristotle, Niccolò Machiavelli, Thomas Hobbes, John Locke, Jean-Jacques Rousseau, Montesquieu, Alexis de Tocqueville, John Stuart Mill, Karl Marx, and other political philosophers
  • Writings, speeches, letters, and published works of Filipino figures such as Dr. José Rizal, Apolinario Mabini, Manuel L. Quezon, Ramon Magsaysay, Corazon Aquino, Benigno Aquino Jr., and other notable Philippine leaders
  • Official speeches, historical records, government archives, and public records
  • Encyclopaedia Britannica (background information on historical figures)
  • Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy (philosophical background and thinker profiles)
  • Oxford Dictionary of Quotations and other recognized quotation references
  • Historical biographies, Political Science textbooks, Constitutional Law references, and peer-reviewed academic publications

Note: The Political Wisdom Library contains quotations from numerous historical figures, political leaders, philosophers, jurists, and public officials across different countries and historical periods. Because the collection is continuously expanded and updated, no single reference applies to every quotation. Whenever appropriate, quotations are cross-referenced with authoritative sources, while some entries are simplified or abbreviated for educational presentation without changing their intended meaning.

📖 Political Dictionary

The Political Dictionary is an educational glossary developed to help students understand key concepts, institutions, principles, laws, theories, ideologies, and terminology commonly encountered in Political Science and related disciplines. Definitions are compiled and adapted from official government publications, constitutional and legal references, and recognized educational reference sources. Each entry includes a definition, a simplified explanation, a practical example, and related concepts to support learning and understanding.

  • Official Gazette of the Republic of the Philippines
  • LawPhil Project – Philippine Laws and Jurisprudence
  • Supreme Court of the Philippines – E-Library
  • Encyclopaedia Britannica
  • Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy
  • United Nations Digital Library

Note:This dictionary covers a broad range of Political Science topics, including Philippine government, constitutional law, governance, public administration, elections, political theory, comparative politics, international relations, public policy, human rights, law, and research methods. Because the dictionary contains hundreds of entries across multiple subject areas, no single reference applies to every definition. Definitions are summarized and adapted for educational purposes while preserving their original concepts and meanings. Examples are provided to help explain the practical application of each term and are intended for educational purposes.
